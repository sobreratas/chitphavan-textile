About

I'm Chitphavanh, an artist from Sam Neua, Huaphan in the Northern part of Laos. My mom taught me to weave when I was just five years old. As the only girl in my family, it was my role to continue this cherished skill. When I was ten, our family moved to Vientiane, and I began using my weaving to earn money for my education, buying textbooks and paying my tuition from the fourth grade onwards.

I would wake up at 4 AM every day to weave, a practice I continued until I finished high school. Following my mother's traditional belief that a high education wasn't necessary for a woman, I stopped my studies. However, I never stopped weaving. Instead, I continued to earn money to support my two younger brothers so they could both complete their bachelor's degrees.

In 2009, I met my husband, a Japanese man working in Laos. With a small budget I managed to save, I was able to build my very own loom and officially become the artist, Chitphavanh.
